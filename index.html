I am a first-year computer science PhD student at UT Austin, broadly interested in theoretical computer science and mathematics. I am fortunate to be advised by Dana Moshkovitz.

Research interests:
  • Pseudorandomness
  • Hardness of Approximation
  • Graph expansion in computer science (small-set expansion, high-dimensional expansion, spectral/threshold-rank algorithms, etc)
I received by bachelor's degree in Applied Mathematics from UC Berkeley in May 2019. My senior thesis was on the algebraic structure of the sandpile group of a graph, under Nikhil Srivastava. I also worked on algorithmic fairness and controls theory, as you can see below.
