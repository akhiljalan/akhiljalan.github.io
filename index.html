I am a first-year computer science PhD student at UT Austin, and a member of the theory group. I am fortunate to be advised by Dana Moshkovitz.
Research interests:
  • Pseudorandomness
  • Hardness of Approximation
  • Expander graphs in all their guises (small-set expansion, high-dimensional expansion, spectral algorithms, Cayley expanders, etc). More generally, the "structured vs pseudorandom" paradigm.
I received by bachelor's degree in Applied Mathematics from UC Berkeley in May 2019. My senior thesis was on the algebraic structure of the sandpile group of a graph, under Nikhil Srivastava. I also worked on algorithmic fairness and controls theory, as you can see below.
